About

Bharatanātyam is one of the most ancient dance styles and is considered unique in having an unbroken tradition for many years. This dance form originated 3000 years ago, with a mythological and a historical foundation. The dance style involves intricate footwork, hand gestures, and facial expressions which are performed in rhythm to classical Indian music. Bharatanātyam is a combination of sequences of pure dance and expressional compositions. It has been aptly said that Bharatanātyam is a symbol of beauty and aesthetic perfection. As a science, it attains the perfection of body technique and corporal movement. As poetry, it is the symbol of rhythmic lyricism of Indian History. As a religion, it is the man's quest for the Supreme and it represents the desire to unite with the Ultimate. As a philosophy, it is a search of human soul for the ideal.

Gurus

Meet our exceptional instructors!

Guru Smt. Daksha Kantaria

Founder & Instructor

Guru Smt. Daksha Kantaria is a founder of Swara Dance Academy. She has been teaching this art form in India and the United States since 1985. Daksha started her initial training in Bharata Natyam at age of eight under Guru Sri. Nattu. Later she studied under late Guru Sri. Madhu Patel and late Guru Sri. Kubernath Tanjorekar, a renowned Bharata Naytam exponent of Gujarat. She had received her training in Nattuvangam from Guru Smt. Kausalya Srinivasan and her Visharad /B.A. in Music in Bharata Natyam from Gandharva Maha Vidyalaya, India. At the age of 14, she was awarded and honored by Gujarat State Government for her accomplishments in this dance form. She has choreographed and organized several stage performances in Classical and Folk dances. Daksha has a Master’s Degree in Accounting, a Degree in Computer Science and MBA in Technology Management. Daksha continues with her passion for dance while working as a technology executive at an insurance company. She also serves on a board as the President for a local chapter of QAI worldwide. She is an active member of Indian-American community in Connecticut and an active volunteer for few non-profit institutions.

Guru Smt. Jeevi Kumaresan

Co-Instructor

Guru Smt. Jeevi Kumaresan joined Swara Natyalaya in 2016 as an eager student in pursuit to continue with her passion of Dance. Shortly thereafter her potential was recognized, and she joined as a co-teacher at Swara Natyalaya and has been teaching Bharatanatyam since then. Jeevi started learning this dance form at a age of five from Guru Smt. Ranganayagi Jayaraman (chennai, TN- India). She was awarded “Natya Thilagam” at her Arangetram in 2009. She has been learning, teaching and performing on-stage since then. Jeevi completed her Pediatric Residency at St. Joseph’s, NJ and is practicing full time pediatrician for the last 10 years.
